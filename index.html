What is nutrition counseling?

I bring my nutrition knowledge to the table, you bring your real life barriers to diet change. Equally as important as learning what eat or avoid, I help you turn your nutrition thoughts into action. Nutrition counseling is a collaborative process where we find realistic and practical methods to improve the nutrition in your life while making sure the food still tastes good.

About Me

Hi! My name is Kimaya and this nutrition consultation service is for clients who want to reach their health goals without giving up flavorful meals. I appreciate the diversity of cuisines and understand there is no "one size fits all" diet. The good news is that taste and nutrition are not exclusive concepts; I'll show you how to bring them together to achieve success.

Background

Kimaya Joshi combined her interests in food science, biochemistry, and education to graduate from Saint Louis University with a Bachelor of Science in Nutrition and Dietetics as well as minors in Public Health and Interdisciplinary Healthcare Education (the first of its kind in the nation). She completed her dietetic residency at the Veterans Health Administration Hospital in Chicagoland and passed her board exam to become a Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN). Since then she has moved to sunny Los Angeles and continues to provide medical nutrition therapy to patients in a local hospital specializing in cardiopulmonary conditions, diabetes management, and geriatrics.

Aside from academic experience, she co-created the Junior Green Youth Farm at the Roberti Community House with support from the Chicago Botanic Gardens and in a separate program helped to implement physical activity curriculum into school districts (elementary, middle, and high schools). She has also worked in the food industry for 6+ years. From boutique and plant based restaurants to hospital tray lines and delivery, she is well seasoned with food in all stages of farm to table.

FAQ

Q: How can a Registered Dietitian Nutritionist help me?

The top concerns my clients seek counseling for:

  • how to follow an alternative diet without missing nutrients ("how do I follow a ___ diet the right way")
  • how to decrease high blood pressure
  • how to decrease bad cholesterol and increase good cholesterol
  • how to gain weight
  • how to lose weight
  • how to improve diabetes management
  • how to prevent pre-diabetes from progressing into diabetes
  • how to eat well on the go
  • how to eat well on a budget
  • what to eat when there are multiple health conditions
  • what to feed picky eaters (kids, elders, or spouses)
  • how to improve nutrition without abandoning traditional cultural foods
  • how to slow the progression of kidney disease
  • what to eat pre/post workout
  • how quickly identify “good” vs “bad” foods when grocery shopping
  • clarify food facts from myths/speculation

If you looking for help with a condition/concern not listed, feel free to email me anyways. If I don't experience in that area, I will be happy to refer you to another Registered Dietitian Nutritionist who does.

Q: What can I expect at my consultation?

A: We will establish your health and wellness goals, review your current diet in detail, identify the most effective changes to make, discuss obstacles you may face to making these changes, and provide solutions to overcome those barriers. All this will be done within your comfort level and will take into account your health beliefs and cultural/alternative diet preferences.

Q: Why are your rates so much lower than local competitors?

A: I believe research based nutrition education should be a right, not a privilege. Everyone deserves access to a RDN (the nutrition healthcare professional), regardless of socioeconomic status.

Q: What if I am looking for a meal plan and no counseling?

A: In my experience, clients who receive a meal plan without counseling have the least long-term success. This is true of most meal plans found online. It is typically followed strictly for some period of time (usually 2-3 weeks) and then abandoned because it doesn’t work with your real life. When we meet for counseling, I create an individualized meal plan to fit your lifestyle (time, finances, cultural background, cooking skill, work/school schedule, etc).

That being said, I understand everyone does not have the means for counseling and/or it may be difficult to speak to a stranger comfortably. If you are confident that you prefer the route of meal plan only, please email me and I would be happy to discuss the logistics and pricing details.

Q: Why should I consult with a Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN) vs. a nutritionist?

A: In short, all Registered Dietitians are nutritionists but all nutritionists are not Registered Dietitians. In some states you can call yourself a nutritionist without any formal nutrition education, training, licensing, or certification. In all states it is illegal to call yourself a Registered Dietitian without proper credentialing. To become a Registered Dietitian, the minimum requirements include: a bachelor’s degree with completion of extensive nutrition curriculum, at least 1,200 hours of supervised practice, a comprehensive registration exam, and 75 continuing professional education credits per five-year cycle.
